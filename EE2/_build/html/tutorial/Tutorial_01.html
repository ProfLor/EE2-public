Tutorial 01#

Repetition of Matrix Calculus#

The purpose of this tutorial is to repeat basic matrix calculus operations. The following 2 x 2 matrices are provided in general form:

\[\begin{split} M_1= \begin{bmatrix} A&B\\ C&D \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
\[\begin{split} M_2= \begin{bmatrix} E&F\\ G&H \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
\[\begin{split} M_3= \begin{bmatrix} I&J\\ K&L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
\[\begin{split} M_4= \begin{bmatrix} D&-B\\ -C&A \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

Exercise 1: Matrix addition#

  1. Calculate the following sums:

    \(M_1+M_4\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1+M_4= \begin{bmatrix} A+D&0\\ 0&D+A \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_2+M_3\):

    \[\begin{split} M_2+M_3= \begin{bmatrix} E+I&F+J\\ G+K&H+L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  2. Calculate following differences:

    \(M_1-M_4\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1-M_4= \begin{bmatrix} A-D&2\,B\\ 2\, C&D-A \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_2-M_3\):

    \[\begin{split}M_2-M_3= \begin{bmatrix} E-I&F-J\\ G-K&H-L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

Exercise 2: Matrix multiplication#

  1. Calculate the determinants of all matrices:

    \(\det{M_1}\):

    \[ \det{M_1} = A \cdot D - B \cdot \]

    \(\det{M_2}\):

    \[ \det{M_2} = E \cdot H - F \cdot G \]

    \(\det{M_3}\):

    \[ \det{M_3} = I \cdot L - J \cdot K \]

    \(\det{M_4}\):

    \[ \det{M_4} = D \cdot A + B \cdot C \]

  2. Calculate the following products:

    \(M_1\cdot M_2\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1\cdot M_2= \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot E + B \cdot G & A \cdot F + B \cdot H \\ C \cdot E + D \cdot G & C \cdot F + D \cdot H \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_1\cdot M_3\):

    \[\begin{split}M_1 \cdot M_3 = \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot I + B \cdot K & A \cdot J + B \cdot L \\ C \cdot I + D \cdot K & C \cdot J + D \cdot L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_2\cdot M_3\):

    \[\begin{split} M_2 \cdot M_3 = \begin{bmatrix} E \cdot I + F \cdot K & E \cdot J + F \cdot L \\ G \cdot I + H \cdot K & G \cdot J + H \cdot L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_3\cdot M_2\):

    \[\begin{split} M_3 \cdot M_2 = \begin{bmatrix} E \cdot I + G \cdot J & F \cdot I + H \cdot J \\ E \cdot K + G \cdot L & F \cdot K + H \cdot L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_1\cdot M_4\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1 \cdot M_4 = \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot D - B \cdot C & 0 \\ 0 & A \cdot D - B \cdot C \end{bmatrix} = \det M_1 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_4\cdot M_1\):

    \[\begin{split} M_4 \cdot M_1 = \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot D - B \cdot C & 0 \\ 0 & A \cdot D - B \cdot C \end{bmatrix} = \det M_4 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  3. What do you notice about the products in exercise 2.2?

    In general, matrix products are not commutative, i. e., \(M_\mathrm{i}\cdot M_\mathrm{j} \neq M_\mathrm{i}\cdot M_\mathrm{i}\). One exepction is the product of a matrix with its inverse (here: \(M_1\cdot M_4\) and \(M_4 \cdot M_1\)), which results in the identity matrix.

  4. Compare the two products \(\left(M_1\cdot M_2\right)\cdot M_3\) and \(M_1\cdot \left(M_2\cdot M_3\right)\).

    \(\left(M_1\cdot M_2\right)\cdot M_3\):

    \[\begin{split} \left(M_1\cdot M_2\right)\cdot M_3\\ = \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot E + B \cdot G & A \cdot F + B \cdot H \\ C \cdot E + D \cdot G & C \cdot F + D \cdot H \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} I & J \\ K & L \end{bmatrix} \\ = \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot E \cdot I + A \cdot F \cdot K + B \cdot G \cdot I + B \cdot H \cdot K & A \cdot E \cdot J + A \cdot F \cdot L + B \cdot G \cdot J + B \cdot H \cdot L \\ C \cdot E \cdot I + C \cdot F \cdot K + D \cdot G \cdot I + D \cdot H \cdot K & C \cdot E \cdot J + C \cdot F \cdot L + D \cdot G \cdot J + D \cdot H \cdot L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_1\cdot \left(M_2\cdot M_3\right)\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1\cdot \left(M_2\cdot M_3\right)\\ = \begin{bmatrix} A & B \\ C & D \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} E \cdot I + F \cdot K & E \cdot J + F \cdot L \\ G \cdot I + H \cdot K & G \cdot J + H \cdot L \end{bmatrix} \\ = \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot E \cdot I + B \cdot G \cdot I + A \cdot F \cdot K + B \cdot H \cdot K & A \cdot E \cdot J + B \cdot G \cdot J + A \cdot F \cdot L + B \cdot H \cdot L \\ C \cdot E \cdot I + D \cdot G \cdot I + C \cdot F \cdot K + D \cdot H \cdot K & C \cdot E \cdot J + D \cdot G \cdot J + C \cdot F \cdot L + D \cdot H \cdot L \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  5. What do you notice about the products in exercise 2.3?

    The products are equal, i. e., \(\left(M_1\cdot M_2\right)\cdot M_3 = M_1\cdot \left(M_2\cdot M_3\right)\).

Exercise 3: Matrix inversion#

  1. For all four matrices \(M_1\) to \(M_4\) calculate the inverse matrices.

    \(M_1^{-1}\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1^{-1}= \frac{1}{\det{M_1}} \begin{bmatrix} D&-B\\ -C&A \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_2^{-1}\):

    \[\begin{split} M_2^{-1}= \frac{1}{\det{M_2}} \begin{bmatrix} H&-F\\ -G&E \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_3^{-1}\):

    \[\begin{split} M_3^{-1}= \frac{1}{\det{M_3}} \begin{bmatrix} L&-J\\ -K&I \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_4^{-1}\):

    \[\begin{split} > M_4^{-1}= > \frac{1}{\det{M_4}} > \begin{bmatrix} > A&-B\\ > -C&D > \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  2. What are the results of \(M_1\cdot M_1^{-1}\) and \(M_1^{-1}\cdot M_1\)?

    \(M_1\cdot M_1^{-1}\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1\cdot M_1^{-1}= \frac{1}{\det{M_1}} \begin{bmatrix} A\cdot D-B\cdot C&0\\ 0&A\cdot D-B\cdot C \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1&0\\ 0&1 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

    \(M_1^{-1}\cdot M_1\):

    \[\begin{split} M_1^{-1}\cdot M_1= \frac{1}{\det{M_1}} \begin{bmatrix} A \cdot D - B \cdot C & 0\\ 0 & A \cdot D - B \cdot C \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  3. What is the term for the type of matrix you calculated in exercise 3.2?

    The matrix is called the identity matrix.

Exercise 4: Equation systems#

  1. What are the results of the following multiplication operations?

    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} A&B\\\ C&D \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} X\\Y \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} R_1+R_2&R_2\\ R_2&R_2+R_3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} I_1\\ I_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} G_1+G_2&-G_1\\ -G_2&G_2+G_3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} U_1\\ U_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} A&B\\\ C&D \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} X\\Y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} A\cdot X+B\cdot Y\\ C\cdot X+D\cdot Y \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} R_1+R_2&R_2\\ R_2&R_2+R_3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} I_1\\ I_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \left(R_1+R_2\right)\cdot I_1& R_2\cdot I_2\\ R_2\cdot I_1&\left(R_2+R_3\right)\cdot I_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} G_1+G_2&-G_1\\ -G_2&G_2+G_3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} U_1\\ U_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \left(G_1+G_2\right)\cdot U_1-G_1\cdot U_2\\ -G_2\cdot U_1+\left(G_2+G_3\right)\cdot U_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  2. Please expand the following equation systems into ordinary equations:

    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} U_1\\U_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} R_1+R_2&R_2\\ R_2&R_2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} I_1\\ I_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} I_1\\ I_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} G_1&-G_1\\ -G_1&G_1+G_2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} U_1\\ U_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{align*} U_1&=\left(R_1+ R_2\right)\cdot I_1+R_2\cdot I_2\\ U_2&=R_2\cdot I_1+R_2\cdot I_2 \end{align*} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{align*} I_1&=G_1\cdot U_1-G_1\cdot U_2\\ I_2&=-G_1\cdot U_1+\left(G_1+G_2\right)\cdot U_2 \end{align*} \end{split}\]

  3. Write the following equations in matrix notation. The voltages U1 and U2 and the currents I1 and I2 should be represented as column vectors which are connected by a resistance or an admittance matrix (see exercise 4.2):

    \[\begin{split} \begin{align*} U_1&=R_1\cdot I_1+R_2\cdot I_1+R_2\cdot I_2\\ U_2&=R_2\cdot I_2+R_3\cdot I_2+R_2\cdot I_1 & \end{align*} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{align*} I_1&=G_1\cdot U_1+G_2\cdot \left(U_1-U_2\right)\\ I_2&=G_3\cdot U_2+G_2\cdot \left( U_2- U_1\right) \end{align*} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} U_1\\ U_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} R_1+R_2&R_2\\ R_2&R_2+R_3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} I_1\\ I_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} I_1\\ I_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} G_1+G_2&-G_2\\ -G_1&G_2+G_3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} U_1\\ U_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  4. Invert the resistance matrix and the admittance matrix of exercise 4.2:

    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} R_1+R_2&R_2\\ R_2&R_2 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} G_1&-G_1\\ -G_1&G_1+G_2 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} R_1+R_2&R_2\\ R_2&R_2 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{R_1\cdot R_2} \begin{bmatrix} R_2&-R_2\\ -R_2&R_1+R_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} G_1 &-G_1\\ -G_1&G_1+G_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
    \[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} G_1&-G_1\\ -G_1&G_1+G_2 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{G_1\cdot G_2} \begin{bmatrix} G_1+G_2&G_1\\ G_1&G_1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} R_1+R_2&R_2\\ R_2&R_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

  5. Compare the inverse resistance matrix with the original admittance matrix, and the inverse admittance matrix with the original resistance matrix.

    The inverse resistance matrix is the same as the original conductance matrix, and the inverse conductance matrix is the same as the original resistance matrix.
